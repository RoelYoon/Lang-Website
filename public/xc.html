Dear XC/TF Bros,


There's always been a moment in every season while I am at the very limit of my physical endurance that I think to myself, I never want to experience this again.

But, as you know, I always come back the next season, ready to endure it all again. I don't come back for the shin splints, medals, plaques, pins, or, really, even for the sport. The feeling of winning is nice, but if that were all there was to it, I wouldn't have returned after my first "K". The only thing that makes these sports worth it is you.

You've given me more laughter and endurance than anybody else. Even when we're under those hellish weathers during practice or when we're shivering under our jacket-blankets at those post-sundown meets, I'm able to smile, knowing I'm making the most memorable moments of my life because I am with you. Even in those times when I feel as if I couldn't possibly smile at all, you somehow manage to lift my lips, lightening everything. And when it seemed like there was just too much and I said I couldn't endure anymore that one night, you promised me, "we'll get through it together."

We really have been through it together. We must have ran 50 marathons at this point. Not every moment was a high. Actually, most of it was straight-up painful. But I would do it all over again, and I will do it over again next year... as long as you are there.

To many more miles (and shin splints), Roel
